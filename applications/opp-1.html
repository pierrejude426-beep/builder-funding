Permitted Purpose

Phare AI's research activities are conducted to create new or improved business components in the form of agentic operations systems. The research directly supports the development of proprietary multi-agent orchestration technologies, automated eligibility-reasoning systems, and advanced prompt engineering frameworks that enable organizations to adopt AI solutions. These research efforts aim to advance the state of artificial intelligence applications in operational environments, specifically targeting grant automation for nonprofits and funding operations for early-stage companies.

Technological in Nature

The research relies fundamentally on principles of computer science, artificial intelligence, and distributed systems engineering. Core technological elements include: (1) Multi-agent orchestration architecture that coordinates multiple AI models (Anthropic, OpenAI, Perplexity) within complex workflows, (2) Novel eligibility-reasoning algorithms that automatically assess qualification criteria across diverse funding programs, (3) Advanced retrieval systems integrated with n8n, Supabase, and Vercel infrastructure, and (4) Proprietary prompt engineering systems that optimize AI model performance for operational tasks. The research applies systematic computer science methodologies to solve complex problems in AI system orchestration and automated decision-making.

Elimination of Uncertainty

The research addresses significant technical uncertainties inherent in multi-agent AI systems: (1) How to effectively orchestrate multiple large language models to work cohesively within operational workflows without conflicts or redundancies, (2) How to develop reliable eligibility-reasoning that accurately interprets complex, multi-criteria funding requirements across different domains, (3) How to optimize retrieval performance and accuracy when integrating multiple data sources and AI models in real-time operational environments, and (4) How to create generalizable prompt systems that maintain performance across diverse operational contexts while preserving system reliability and user experience.

Process of Experimentation

Phare AI employs systematic experimentation methodologies including: (1) Iterative architecture testing with controlled deployment of different multi-agent configurations to measure coordination effectiveness and system reliability, (2) A/B testing of eligibility-reasoning algorithms against known qualification datasets to optimize accuracy and reduce false positives/negatives, (3) Performance benchmarking of retrieval systems under varying load conditions and data complexity scenarios, (4) Prompt engineering experimentation using systematic variation and measurement of output quality, consistency, and task completion rates, and (5) Live deployment testing with actual client systems (ICF grant automation, hospitality and dental voice concierge agents) to validate research outcomes in production environments. All experimentation follows documented protocols with measurable success criteria and iterative refinement processes.
